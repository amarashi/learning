# 0001 — Mission set; zero formal design vocabulary, strong systems thinking

**Date:** 2026-06-28

## Who / prior knowledge
Same learner as the `../web-dev/` course. Strong technical/analytical background
(MATLAB, R, Python; ML day-job). Reasons in systems, rules and models. He *makes*
things — web (FHA site), charts, slides — and can feel when something is off, but
has **no formal design vocabulary or framework**: can't name *why* a layout works or
how to fix it. Treat him as a smart beginner in this domain specifically.

## Mission (set this session — see [[MISSION.md]])
Train the **eye and the vocabulary first** — concepts, terms, ideas — **tool-agnostic**.
Production/tool mastery comes later. Three drivers, in his words: (1) make his own
graphics, (2) judge & brief design work, (3) lift the look of his web projects.
Practice happens in **Procreate** (iPad): every skill lesson ends with a small
before/after task he can actually run.

## Teaching approach that should work (high confidence)
- **Diagrams/maps first**, then detail — confirmed preference, carried from web-dev.
- Frame design as **rules/systems**, not "taste" — plays to his strengths. The
  *principles* framing is the spine.
- Short lessons, one tangible win, retrieval + spacing over memorisation.
- Don't re-teach general logic/structure; *do* teach domain-specific perception
  (value vs colour, Gestalt grouping, hierarchy).

## Curriculum spine chosen
1. **Elements vs Principles** map — orientation (Lesson 0001, *delivered*).
2. **C.R.A.P.** as first actionable toolkit (Proximity+Alignment → Contrast+Hierarchy).
3. **Gestalt** (why grouping works) → then per-element deep-dives: **Type, Colour
   (value before hue), Space/Hierarchy**.

## State after this session
Lesson 01 (the map) *delivered, not yet demonstrated* — no retention evidence yet.
**Next session:** confirm the elements-vs-principles split stuck (have him sort a few
unfamiliar terms / critique a real piece naming both), check whether he ran the
Procreate proximity+contrast exercise and what he noticed, then drill into the first
principle pair — likely **Proximity & Alignment** (fastest "instant tidiness" win).
Watch for: does the *value vs colour* idea need its own moment? He may reach for hue
when value is the real lever.
