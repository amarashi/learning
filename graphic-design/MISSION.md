# Mission: A Designer's Eye

## Why
Amir keeps *making* things — web projects (the FHA site), charts, slides, the odd
graphic — but he was never taught to *see* design. He can tell when something looks
off, not **why**, and can't reliably make it right. The goal is to build the
underlying literacy: the concepts, terms and ideas behind why a design works, so he
can **make his own graphics with intent, judge & brief design work, and lift the
look of everything he builds.**

This stage is about the **eye and the vocabulary**, not any one tool. Procreate is
the practice gym, not the subject.

## Success looks like
- Look at any design (a poster, a slide, a UI, a chart) and name **what's working,
  what's off, and how to fix it** — in real design vocabulary.
- Separate the **elements** (the raw materials) from the **principles** (the rules
  for arranging them), and point to both in a real piece.
- Make a deliberate composition in Procreate and **predict its effect** before
  anyone sees it — "this reads first because of contrast; these group because of
  proximity."
- Give a designer (or an AI) feedback that names the principle, not just "make it
  pop."

## Constraints
- Strong analytical/technical background (MATLAB / R / Python; ML day-job). Thinks in
  systems and rules — design *principles* will land harder than "taste."
- Learns best **diagrams & maps first**, detail second. Lead every lesson with a visual.
- Time-boxed: short lessons, one tangible win each. No memorisation drills.
- **Tool-agnostic concepts**, but practice tasks should be runnable in **Procreate**
  (iPad) so learning is hands-on and tested, not just read.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Tool/software mastery (Figma, Illustrator, Procreate technique) — production skills
  come *after* the eye is trained.
- Brand strategy, motion, UX research, print production specs — name them later, don't
  drill now.
- Drawing/illustration craft as such (Procreate is the canvas, not a sketching course).
