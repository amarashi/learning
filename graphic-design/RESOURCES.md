# Resources

High-trust resources behind the graphic-design lessons. Verified live 2026-06-28.

## Primary references (the canon)

- **The Non-Designer's Design Book — Robin Williams** (book)
  The classic that named the **C.R.A.P.** principles (Contrast, Repetition, Alignment,
  Proximity). The single best beginner text for *non-designers who make things*. Our
  default citation for the four core arranging principles.
  *Trust: highest (the foundational popular text).*

- **Interaction Design Foundation — Gestalt Principles** —
  https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/gestalt-principles
  Rigorous, well-cited explainer of how the eye groups what it sees (proximity,
  similarity, closure, common region, figure/ground, continuity). Updated 2026.
  *Trust: highest.* Default citation for perception/grouping.

- **Interaction Design Foundation — Visual Hierarchy & the elements/principles** —
  https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/visual-hierarchy
  Solid reference for hierarchy, contrast, and how principles direct attention.

- **Thinking with Type — Ellen Lupton** — https://thinkingwithtype.com/
  The standard accessible reference on typography (letter, text, grid). For when we
  reach Type. *Trust: highest for type.*

## Orientation / good explainers

- **Canva Design School — Design Basics** — https://www.canva.com/learn/design/
  Free, friendly, well-illustrated articles on elements, principles, colour, type.
  Good for a second angle and lots of examples. *Trust: high (commercial but solid).*

- **Saylor Academy — C.R.A.P. Principles (open courseware)** —
  https://saylordotorg.github.io/text_business-information-systems-design-an-app-for-that/s07-01-c-r-a-p-principles-of-graphic-.html
  Free, plain-language walkthrough of the four principles. Good printable backup.

- **Presentation Zen — Design fundamentals (CRAP)** —
  https://presentationzen.com/blog/design-fundamentals-contrast-repetition-alignment-proximity
  Garr Reynolds applying CRAP to slides — relevant to Amir's charts/decks driver.

## Video / wisdom (practitioners)

- **The Futur (YouTube)** — https://www.youtube.com/@thefutur
  Chris Do & co. Working designers explaining design thinking, type, critique. Good
  for the *wisdom* layer — how pros actually reason and give feedback.

## Communities (for wisdom / asking real people)

- **r/graphic_design** — https://www.reddit.com/r/graphic_design/ — broad, active,
  beginner questions welcome; good for "is this any good / why" critique.
- **r/Procreate** — https://www.reddit.com/r/Procreate/ — for the hands-on practice
  side once we're making things in the tool.
- **r/Design_Critique** — https://www.reddit.com/r/Design_Critique/ — post work, get
  structured feedback. The real-world test of a trained eye.

## The practice gym
- **Procreate** (iPad). Tool-agnostic concepts get tested here with small before/after
  exercises. Not the subject of study — the place we *apply* it.
